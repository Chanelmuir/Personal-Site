## Explanation
**What is Torrenting?**  
Torrenting is a peer-to-peer (P2P) method of sharing files. Instead of downloading from a single server, you download small pieces of a file from many other users ("peers") while simultaneously uploading pieces you've already downloaded.

**Key Terms:**  
Leeching (Downloading): Receiving pieces of the file from other users.  
Seeding (Uploading): Sharing completed pieces with other users.

## Torrenting Tips
When torrenting files, there are many features that can enhance your experience. Here are a few I find helpful:

- **Peers**: On this tab you can see all the people sharing the files with each other. As you can see, your IP Address will be visible to everyone. Since torrenting is peer-to-peer, other peers can see your public IP address. Some people choose to use a VPN for additional privacy.

- **Content**: On this tab you can select/deselect files as you could earlier. Additionally you can set the priority of certain files. Sometimes you're downloading a folder with many files. If you want to start with a specific file, you can make that one download with a higher priority to minimise your waiting time. Right click on individual files and adjust the priority using the menu.
